Engineering Summary

The Loan Management System (LMS) is designed to streamline loan origination, servicing, and management processes. It leverages a microservices architecture to ensure scalability, maintainability, and resilience. The system utilizes REST APIs for inter-service communication and message queues for asynchronous processing. Key technologies include Node.js with Express.js for the backend, PostgreSQL for the database, and AWS for cloud infrastructure. Security measures include OAuth 2.0 with JWT for authentication, Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) for authorization, and AES-256 encryption for data protection. The system is designed to handle increasing loan volume and user base while maintaining high performance and availability.

Backend Architecture Overview

The LMS follows a microservices architecture pattern, where each service is responsible for a specific business capability. Services communicate through REST APIs and message queues, ensuring loose coupling and high cohesion. The architecture is designed to be scalable, maintainable, and resilient, with each service independently deployable and upgradable. Key design principles include separation of concerns, single responsibility, and loose coupling.

Engineering Risks

Data Breach Risk

Description: Unauthorized access to sensitive customer and financial data. Impact: Loss of customer trust and potential legal consequences. Mitigation: Implement robust security measures, including encryption, access controls, and regular security audits.

System Downtime Risk

Description: Prolonged system unavailability due to technical issues or external factors. Impact: Disruption of loan management processes and loss of revenue. Mitigation: Implement high availability and disaster recovery strategies, including redundant systems and regular backups.

Regulatory Non-Compliance Risk

Description: Failure to adhere to relevant regulatory requirements. Impact: Legal penalties and reputational damage. Mitigation: Regularly review and update compliance measures, including automated compliance checks and regular audits.

Performance Degradation Risk

Description: Deterioration of system performance due to increasing load or technical issues. Impact: Slower response times and reduced user satisfaction. Mitigation: Implement performance monitoring and optimization strategies, including load testing and performance tuning.

Integration Failure Risk

Description: Failure of external integrations (e.g., credit bureau, payment gateway) to function correctly. Impact: Disruption of loan management processes and potential financial losses. Mitigation: Implement robust integration testing and monitoring, including fallback mechanisms and regular integration audits.
