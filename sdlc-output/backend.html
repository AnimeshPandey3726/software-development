Hospitable Management System

Microservices architecture with REST APIs and Webhooks for integrations.

Technology Stack: React, Node.js with Express.js, PostgreSQL, AWS (EC2, RDS, S3), REST APIs, Webhooks, OAuth 2.0, JWT, HTTPS

Executive Summary: The Hospitable Management System (HMS) is designed to streamline operations for hospitality businesses, encompassing booking management, guest services, and financial reporting. This system aims to enhance guest satisfaction, optimize resource utilization, and improve overall operational efficiency, ultimately driving revenue growth and competitive advantage.

Engineering Summary

The backend architecture follows a microservices pattern, utilizing REST APIs and Webhooks for integrations. The system is designed to be scalable, secure, and maintainable, with a focus on delivering high performance and compliance with industry standards such as PCI DSS and GDPR.

Backend Architecture Overview

The architecture pattern is microservices, with each service responsible for a specific business capability. The system uses REST APIs for communication between services and Webhooks for external integrations. The design principles include loose coupling, high cohesion, and independent deployability.

Engineering Risks

Data Breach Risk

Description: Unauthorized access to sensitive guest data. Impact: Compromise of guest privacy and potential legal consequences. Mitigation: Implement strong authentication and authorization controls, encrypt sensitive data, and conduct regular security audits.

System Downtime Risk

Description: Unexpected system downtime due to hardware failure or network issues. Impact: Loss of revenue and customer dissatisfaction. Mitigation: Implement redundant systems, conduct regular maintenance, and have a disaster recovery plan in place.

Integration Failure Risk

Description: Failure of external integrations (e.g., payment gateway, email service) to function correctly. Impact: Disruption of business operations and customer dissatisfaction. Mitigation: Implement robust error handling and fallback mechanisms, and regularly test integrations.

Performance Degradation Risk

Description: Degradation of system performance due to increased load or inefficient algorithms. Impact: Slow response times and poor user experience. Mitigation: Monitor system performance, optimize algorithms, and scale resources as needed.

Compliance Risk

Description: Failure to comply with industry regulations (e.g., PCI DSS, GDPR). Impact: Legal penalties and loss of customer trust. Mitigation: Stay updated with regulatory requirements, conduct regular compliance audits, and implement necessary controls.
